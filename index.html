What Hydroponics

Hydroponics is a sort of horticulture and a subset of hydroculture that includes utilizing mineral fertilizer solutions in an aqueous solvent to grow plants, mainly crops, without soil.

Hydroponics has a lot of benefits, one of which is that it saves water in agriculture. To cultivate 1 kilogramm (2.2 lb) of tomatoes using intensive farming methods, 400L (88 imp gal; 110 U.S. gal) of water is required; hydroponics requires 70L and aeroponics requires just 20L (4.4 imp gal; 5.3 U.S. gal). [8 Because hydroponics requires far less water to grow produce, individuals living in harsh areas with limited access to water may be able to cultivate their own food in the future.

Why ufarms

ufarms is a complete farming automation system tuned for highest productivity per unit area by utilizing sensor inputs of environmental parameters like temperature, ph, EC, humidity etc, evaluating it with a crop specific database, to adjust the parameters for maximum productivity. Crop specific data collected through multiple years, the unique and versatile bus model control unit architecture and cloud based processing makes the product unique and innovative.
